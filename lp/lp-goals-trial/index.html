Why don't your goals ever stick?

They're aimed at outcomes, not behavior. We rebuild them as testable behavioral hypotheses, and they hold.

Behavioral science with teams at:

You might recognize the shape.

You set the goal in Q1. By Q3, half the team forgot it existed, and the other half re-interpreted it three times.

  • Goals that read like wishlists, not work. Outcomes named without the behaviors that produce them.
  • Mid-quarter reframes that quietly redefine success. The number on the dashboard meant something different in April than it does in July.
  • Year-end reviews where nobody can name what "on track" meant. The goal is technically met. The work that earned it is invisible.

What's underneath.

The SMART-goal trap.

SMART optimizes for measurability, not the behaviors that produce the outcome. It never models the path, and without the path the goal stays wishful.

Rarely shows up alone. Usually co-occurs with status asymmetry and decision avoidance.

We change the loop, not the symptom.

Half-day with the leadership team. We decompose the goal, design the behavioral tests that would actually move it, and commit to a schedule. The team leaves with goals they can run.
